\section{Project Background and Motivation}
Desertification reversal programs can produce visible greening in the short term, but decision making needs evidence of long-term stability under changing climate and management conditions. This project builds a data-driven dynamical model from satellite observations and then tests future behavior with stochastic simulations and intervention scenarios.

The central scientific question is:

\textbf{Are observed improvements stable attractor shifts, or can they reverse under stress?}

The final phase had four goals.

    \item Ensure numerical stability and physical plausibility of discovered equations.
    \item Quantify collapse risk with multiple metrics, not only endpoint statistics.
    \item Produce a complete report-ready artifact set for academic evaluation.
\end{enumerate}

\section{Methodology}
\subsection{Pipeline overview}
The final modular pipeline includes:
\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=1.6em]
    \item Data extraction and scaling.
    \item Feature engineering and quality filters.
    \item Symbolic regression for $d\mathrm{NDVI}/dt$.
    \item Tiered equation selection with physical checks.
    \item Stochastic simulation with interventions.
    \item Reliability gate and reporting outputs.
\end{enumerate}

\subsection{Feature engineering and quality controls}
Key engineered variables include $\mathrm{NDVI}^2$, normalized rainfall, temperature anomalies, dust stress proxies, and groundwater anomaly proxies.

Final quality controls include:
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=1.4em]
    \item Flat-feature removal.
    \item Zero-heavy feature removal.
    \item Low-correlation filter for weak explanatory signals.
    \item Dynamic recomputation of NDVI-derived terms during simulation.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Governing equation discovery}
The target for symbolic regression is deseasonalized monthly vegetation change:
\begin{equation}
 y_t = \Delta \mathrm{NDVI}_t - \mathbb{E}\left[\Delta \mathrm{NDVI} \mid \text{month}(t)\right].
\end{equation}

The discovered drift model has the form:
\begin{equation}
\frac{d\mathrm{NDVI}}{dt} = f\left(\mathrm{NDVI}, \mathbf{x}_t\right),
\end{equation}
where $\mathbf{x}_t$ is the vector of drivers.

\subsection{Tiered equation selection}
Candidate equations are evaluated by a tier system from strongest to weakest structural confidence.

\begin{table}[H]
\centering
\caption{Tier logic used for equation selection}
\begin{tabular}{p{1.2cm}p{13cm}}
\toprule
\textbf{Tier} & \textbf{Requirement} \\
\midrule
P1 & Stable equilibrium in valid NDVI range with bounded slope and valid sign change \\
P2 & Monotone decreasing drift with valid sign change \\
P2.5 & Stable sign change under strict checks \\
P3 & NDVI-present equation with sign change and no nested trig \\
P4 & NDVI-present equation with sign change \\
P5 & NDVI-present fallback \\
P6 & Absolute fallback by loss \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{table}

\subsection{Reliability gate}
The final reliability gate combines structural and statistical checks.

\textbf{Core checks:}
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=1.4em]
    \item Tier validity.
    \item Fit validity.
    \item Clip saturation validity.
    \item Stable sign-change validity.
\end{itemize}

Fit validity uses two metrics:
\begin{equation}
R^2 = 1 - \frac{\mathrm{Var}(y-\hat{y})}{\mathrm{Var}(y)+\epsilon},
\end{equation}
\begin{equation}
\mathrm{nMAE}_{\mathrm{IQR}} = \frac{\mathrm{MAE}(y,\hat{y})}{Q_{75}(y)-Q_{25}(y)+\epsilon}.
\end{equation}

A region passes fit validity if either $R^2$ is above threshold or $\mathrm{nMAE}_{\mathrm{IQR}}$ is below threshold.

\subsection{Simulation model}
The future trajectory is computed by Euler-Maruyama integration:
\begin{equation}
 v_{t+\Delta t} = \mathrm{clip}\left(v_t + f(v_t,\mathbf{x}_t)\Delta t + \sigma \sqrt{\Delta t}\,\xi_t,\,0,1\right),
\end{equation}
where $\xi_t \sim \mathcal{N}(0,1)$.

\textbf{Horizon:} 50 years. \textbf{Ensemble size:} 150 Monte Carlo simulations per scenario.

\subsection{Collapse metrics}
Three collapse metrics are reported:
\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=1.6em]
    \item \textbf{Ever-collapse probability:} at least one crossing below threshold.
    \item \textbf{Terminal-collapse probability:} endpoint below threshold.
    \item \textbf{Persistent-collapse probability:} below threshold continuously for at least 2 years.
\end{enumerate}

\section{Limitations and Responsible Interpretation}
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=1.4em]
    \item $R^2$ values are still low, especially in Rajasthan.
    \item The Rajasthan selected equation remains a P5 fallback form.
    \item Several intervention levers are not directly represented in the selected Rajasthan equation, so their effect can be indirect.
    \item The model is best interpreted for comparative risk and policy ranking, not as a precise deterministic month-by-month forecast.
\end{itemize}

\textbf{Final interpretation rule used in this report:} The model is accepted as a decision-support model with explicit uncertainty and explicit reliability diagnostics.
